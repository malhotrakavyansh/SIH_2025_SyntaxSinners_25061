Sikkim does not require Indian citizens to obtain any special permit to enter or travel through the main tourist areas of the state, such as Gangtok, Pelling, Ravangla, and Yuksom. This is different from some other northeastern Indian states like Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Mizoram, and Nagaland, which require an Inner Line Permit for all visitors.

However, certain restricted border areas of Sikkim close to the Tibet (China) border do require special permits for all visitors, including Indian citizens. These restricted zones include Nathu La Pass, Tsomgo (Changu) Lake, Yumthang Valley, Gurudongmar Lake, and other parts of North Sikkim. Indian citizens need a Protected Area Permit (PAP) to visit these areas, which is typically arranged by a registered local travel agency in Gangtok using a copy of a government photo ID. Foreign nationals require a Restricted Area Permit (RAP) to enter Sikkim at all, and can generally only visit North Sikkim and other restricted zones as part of a group tour arranged through a registered travel agency; foreign nationals are currently not permitted to visit Nathu La Pass. Because these rules can change, travelers should confirm current permit requirements with a local travel agent or the Sikkim Tourism Department shortly before their trip.

The best time to visit Sikkim for clear mountain views and pleasant weather is generally from March to May (spring, with rhododendrons in bloom) and October to mid-December (autumn, with the clearest skies after the monsoon). The monsoon season, roughly June to September, brings heavy rain, landslide risk on mountain roads, and reduced visibility of peaks like Kanchenjunga, though the landscape turns lush and green. Winter (December to February) is cold, especially at higher elevations like Yumthang and Gurudongmar, where roads can be blocked by snow, but it is a good time for clear views and for those wanting to see snowfall.

Gangtok, the state capital, is the main gateway for most visitors and is well connected by road from Siliguri and Bagdogra Airport in West Bengal (a drive of around 4 to 5 hours). Sikkim also has its own airport, Pakyong, a short drive from Gangtok, with limited flights from cities like Kolkata and Delhi. From Gangtok, most monasteries and attractions are reached by shared jeeps, hired taxis, or organized day tours, since public transport options are limited in the hilly terrain. A few days in Gangtok can comfortably be combined with a loop through West Sikkim (Pelling, Pemayangtse, Yuksom) and South Sikkim (Ravangla, Ralang), while North Sikkim (Lachen, Lachung, Yumthang, Gurudongmar) is usually done as a separate 2-3 day trip due to permit requirements and road conditions.
