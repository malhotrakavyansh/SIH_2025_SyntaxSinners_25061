Phodong Monastery is located in North Sikkim, approximately 28 kilometers from the state capital Gangtok, making it a feasible half-day trip from the city. The current structure was established in 1740 under the patronage of Chogyal Gyurmed Namgyal, though an older monastery had pre-existed on the same site. The 9th Karmapa played a crucial role in establishing three significant monasteries in Sikkim, with Phodong being one of them alongside Rumtek and Ralang. The monastery underwent reconstruction efforts led by Sidkeong Tulku Namgyal, recognized as the reincarnation of his uncle Sidkeong Namgyal, the monastery's abbot, with the spiritual lineage continuing through Palden Thondup Namgyal.

Phodong Monastery belongs to the Kagyupa sect within Tibetan Buddhism, specifically the Karma Kagyu school. The architecture reflects early 18th-century Himalayan Buddhist building traditions, and the monastery houses a notable collection of ancient murals. It maintains an active community of approximately 260 resident monks.

Visitors can witness the Chaam mask dance festival, celebrated on the 28th and 29th of the 10th lunar month, an important ceremonial tradition at the site. Nearby, the ruins of Tumlong, a former capital of Sikkim, and the Labrang Monastery are often visited together with Phodong as part of a North Sikkim day trip from Gangtok.
