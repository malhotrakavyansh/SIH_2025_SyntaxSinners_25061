Ralang Monastery, formally known as Ralong Palchen Choling, is situated in South Sikkim, approximately six kilometers from the town of Ravangla. The original monastery was established in 1768, though the structure visitors encounter today is a more recent construction.

The monastery belongs to the Kagyu school of Tibetan Buddhism. It maintains strong connections to the broader Kagyu hierarchy, with senior religious figures such as the Gyaltsab Rinpoche holding significant leadership roles. The older Ralang Gompa underwent significant reconstruction between 1975 and 1981. In 1995, the 12th Gyaltsab Rinpoche oversaw the construction of a new monastery complex known as the Palchen Choeling Monastic Institute, which retained traditional Tibetan architecture while incorporating modern building techniques. The monastery houses an extensive collection of paintings and thangkas (traditional Buddhist scroll paintings). The older Karma Rabtenling monastery remains situated nearby, west of the new complex.

Ralang serves as a host venue for the annual Pang Lhabsol festival, during which Mount Kangchenjunga receives ritual veneration, typically occurring in September. The festival season extends into early December and culminates with the Kagyed ceremony. The monastery's festival calendar also includes Chaam mask dance performances on the 15th of the 7th month and the 29th of the 10th month, plus Mahakala observances during the 9th month. Its proximity to Ravangla makes it easy to combine a visit with the Buddha Park (Tathagata Tsal) and Ravangla's viewpoints over Kanchenjunga.
